(1997) --- name: outline1 --- ## Outcome determines modeling strategy 1. How many alternatives in the choice set? 2. Are those alternatives ordered? 3. Are those alternatives counts? ??? 1. Number of alternatives - If 2, logit or probit - If more: continue 2. Are they ordered? - Yes: ordered logit or probit - No: continue 3. Counts? - Yes: Poisson or beta-binomial - No: Multinomial logit --- ## Latent variable - Latent variable which is predicted by our model then translated via a link function - The logic is the same as the binary model, but we now have more categories - Thus we're not trying to figure out `\(Pr(y_i=1)\)`, but `\(Pr(y_i=t)\)` for all categories, `\(t\)`, of the outcome - All probabilities have to sum to 1 - This can get very complicated ## Ordered models - Logit - Assumptions - Probit - Assumptions ## Count models - Poisson - Quasi-poisson - Negative binomial - Some others (beta-binomial, various zero-inflated models) ## Multinomial models - Logit - Probit - Conditional models
